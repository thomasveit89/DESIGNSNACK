# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Senior decision-makers at Swiss enterprise companies in banking, insurance, and healthcare — evaluating an embedded Design Engineer for a long-term engagement. They arrive skeptical. They've worked with agencies and freelancers who handed off Figma files. They need proof this is different.

**Secondary:** Builders at scale-ups and digital-first companies building AI products — looking for someone who can move fast without sacrificing craft. They care less about credentials and more about what shipped.

**Context:** Visited quickly, often on desktop, during a procurement or team-building moment. First impression sets the trust ceiling. The site needs to answer "can this person actually build?" without stating it directly.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and positioning platform for Tommy Veit, Design Engineer at DESIGNSNACK GmbH, Romanshorn, Switzerland. 17+ years of UX depth, frontend roots, AI-accelerated workflow.

The strategic shift: from "UX/UI Designer" to "Design Engineer." The site itself is the argument — designed and built by Tommy, not handed off. Success means a senior partner at a Swiss bank reads the hero, scrolls the work, and thinks: "This person is in a different class."

Primary conversion: `tommy@designsnack.ch` — direct email inquiry for embedded engagements or project-based work.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Precise. Curious. Unhurried.

**Voice:** Direct, confident, no filler. Copy carries personality — not decorative elements. Writes the way a senior person talks: no hedging, no hyperbole, no corporate jargon.

**Emotional goal:** Quiet confidence. The site should feel like it was made by someone who didn't need to prove anything — which is exactly how you prove everything.

**Tone:** Personal but not casual. Swiss-precise without being cold. The kind of work where getting the details wrong actually matters.

## Anti-references

- Standard agency portfolio templates — predictable hero + work grid + contact form
- Tailwind UI visual defaults — shadcn components, Inter font, zinc palette, generic radii
- Purple gradient aesthetic — the SaaS/indie hacker default
- Web agency scroll tricks — parallax, scroll hijacking, anything that fights the user
- Generic "modern minimal" — could be any designer, signals nothing
- The hero-metric template — big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent
- Identical card grids — same-sized cards with icon + heading + text, repeated endlessly
- Side-stripe borders on cards — lazy visual accent
- Gradient text — decorative, undermines precision
- Glassmorphism used decoratively

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Every design decision is a demonstration of what a Design Engineer does. If a choice could come from a template, it's the wrong choice.

2. **Typography as primary design element.** Big, confident type beats a complex layout executed safely. When in doubt: do less, with more conviction.

3. **Earn every word.** Copy carries the personality. No restated headings, no introductory paragraphs that repeat the title, no bullet points that could be cut.

4. **Precision signals trust.** The target clients (banking, insurance, healthcare) notice when details are wrong. Spacing, alignment, and motion should be Swiss-precise — not just "clean."

5. **Calm authority, not performance.** No animations that make the user wait. No motion that says "look at me." Entrance animations are polite, not demanding.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Reduced-motion preference respected via `prefers-reduced-motion` media query — all Framer Motion animations wrapped accordingly. Color contrast checked against both light and dark backgrounds. Keyboard navigation supported throughout.
